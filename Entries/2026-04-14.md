 What you're describing:

 Not a note-taking app with search. A living knowledge substrate — every thought you write becomes a node in a growing graph of your mind. The system understands meaning, not
 keywords. Connections form automatically. Your past thoughts inform your present ones without you having to remember them.

 The enrichment layer I'd build:

 Every entry, on save, gets:

  - 1024-dim embedding via mxbai-embed-large — stored in SurrealDB
  - Semantic graph edges — automatically linked to the N most similar past entries by cosine similarity, not by
    tag or keyword
  - Rich metadata beyond what's there now:
    - fluxTitle, category, summary, tags (already exists — keep)
    - entities — people, projects, places detected in the text
    - emotionalTone — are you energized, stuck, anxious, clear?
    - questionsRaised — open questions extracted and tracked across entries
    - momentumSignal — is this topic accelerating or stagnating across your history?
    - temporalRelevance — is this time-sensitive? Does it decay?
    - contradictions — flags when this conflicts with something you wrote before
    - transclusionCandidates — passages from other entries that belong here

 The Xanadu/Roam layer:

  - Every entry shows its backlinks — what previous thoughts connect here
  - Question threads — a question you raised three weeks ago resurfaces when you write something that answers it
  - Momentum map — visualize which topics in your life are alive vs. dead
  - Contradiction detector — "you said X on Feb 3, now you're saying the opposite — is this a pivot or drift?"
